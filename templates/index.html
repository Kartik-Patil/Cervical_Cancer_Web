Let's Talk Statistics

World Cervical Cancer Incidence Rates

Cervical cancer continues to be a global health challenge, with over 660,000 new cases and 350,000 deaths in 2022. The disease is most common in low-income regions due to limited screening and vaccination access. Advances in HPV vaccination have significantly reduced cervical cancer rates, especially in younger women.

Cervical Cancer Frequency

According to WHO Cervical cancer is the most frequent cancer among women, impacting 2.1 million women each year, and also causes the greatest number of cancer-related deaths among women. In 2018, it is estimated that 627,000 women died from cervical cancer – that is approximately 15% of all cancer deaths among women.

Age increases the risk of cervical cancer

The older we are, the more likely abnormal changes will occur in our cells. When many of these changes occur, cancer can develop.

Cancer Mortality rate in Women

Cervical cancer ranks as the third leading cause of cancer-related deaths in women worldwide, claiming a significant number of lives annually. Preventive measures and early detection are crucial to reducing this alarming mortality rate..

How to prevent cervical cancer?

Get regular screenings and HPV vaccination to lower your risk.
